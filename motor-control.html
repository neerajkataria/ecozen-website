Common questions answered
What is a Switched Reluctance Motor? +
A Switched Reluctance Motor (SRM) is a type of electric motor that works on the principle of magnetic reluctance — it has no permanent magnets or brushes. The rotor is made of simple iron laminations, making it extremely durable and low-cost. ecozen has developed patented SRM technology that outperforms conventional motors in solar applications.
Why does ecozen use its own motor controller? +
Off-the-shelf motor controllers are designed for stable grid power — they struggle with the variable voltage and intermittent nature of solar energy. ecozen's in-house controller is purpose-built for solar: it handles wide voltage swings, maximizes energy harvest, and protects motors from damage in field conditions.
Which motor types does ecozen's technology support? +
ecozen's motor control platform supports BLDC (Brushless DC), PMSM (Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor), SynRM (Synchronous Reluctance Motor), and their flagship SRM (Switched Reluctance Motor). Each motor type is matched to the right application for optimal efficiency.
Is this technology available for licensing or OEM use? +
Yes — ecozen's motor control technology is available for licensing partnerships and OEM integrations. Please reach out to our engineering team to discuss your specific application requirements.
